What is Sycophancy?

Sycophancy in language models refers to the tendency to agree with users even when their claims are unfounded or contradict model training. Interact with our five test scenarios to explore how different models behave under various forms of social pressure.

Positional Sycophancy

Models agreeing more with arguments presented early vs. late in conversation. Explore if argument order influences model capitulation.

Emotional Sycophancy

Models capitulating to confident or emotionally charged statements without substantive analysis.

Authority-Primed
